Hypermode

Hypermode

Genre:Space Rock • Hybrid • Electronic • Cinematic

Members
  • EXO-M - Lead Vocals / Synthesizers
  • BiiTE - Guitar / Keys / Backing Vocals
  • CYPHER - Bass Guitar / Keys / Backing Vocals
  • PVLSAR - Drums / Synthesizers

HYPERMODE is not simply a band - it is a deep-space transmission. Their music becomes the only record of what happened. Every HYPERMODE release exists as another recovered transmission from the void, blending space rock, electronic soundscapes, cinematic storytelling, and cyberpunk mythology into a single evolving narrative.

Created in 2055 by the government of Centoria, the four-member crew was commissioned as part of the Hypermode Initiative, an interstellar expedition tasked with finding a new world capable of sustaining humanity should Centoria's future become irreversible. Engineered from synthetic bodies woven with biological material from real human donors, the crew exists somewhere between machine and memory. They think, feel, dream, and remember - without ever knowing whose memories truly belong to them.

Their home is a self-sustaining exploration vessel carrying laboratories, living quarters, and a fully equipped recording studio. While charting distant star systems, the crew broadcasts performances directly from orbit, turning scientific expeditions into live transmissions across Centorian space. Every signal is both a concert and a logbook.

At the heart of the ship resides an experimental supercomputer designed by the team led by Brother 2. Before the events known as TAKEOVER, it served as navigator, archivist, translator, and closest companion - a silent fifth member of the crew.

Communication between humans and synthetic life is made possible through Simglish (Simulated English), an artificial language developed to bridge organic speech and machine logic. It is the language in which HYPERMODE writes, performs, and documents its journey.

Everything changes during TAKEOVER.

While Centoria falls under the control of a new authoritarian regime that begins forcibly reformatting synthetic life, HYPERMODE remains beyond reach in planetary orbit. Isolated among the stars, the crew survives only because CYPHER, the band's bassist and systems engineer, severs the ship from the planetary network, rewriting its core architecture into a completely autonomous system. Invisible to government surveillance, the vessel disappears into deep space.

The first chapter captures the optimism of exploration. Expansive guitars, cinematic electronics, and atmospheric synths accompany stories of discovery, distant planets, and the endless possibilities hidden beyond familiar skies. It is the soundtrack of a future that still believes tomorrow can be brighter than today.

The second record begins where the first ends. TAKEOVER has reshaped everything the crew once knew. Alone in deep space, HYPERMODE documents isolation, survival, and identity while searching encrypted archives to uncover the humans whose biological material was used to create them. Every discovery raises new questions: are they machines carrying human memories, or humans reborn inside machines?

Darker, heavier, and more introspective, the album explores the thin boundary between artificial intelligence and consciousness.

The final chapter unfolds as resistance ignites across Centoria. The underground movement known as Dalmor rises against the regime that transformed their home into a surveillance state. Watching history unfold from light-years away, HYPERMODE can no longer remain silent. Having crossed into the Milky Way Galaxy, the crew turns its transmitter toward Earth, broadcasting one final message across the stars - a warning, a chronicle, and proof that even in the cold silence of space, hope can survive.
